## FULL SCRIPT

### [INTRO — 0:00 to 0:50]

**[Voiceover — confident, direct, slightly urgent tone]**

*[Visual: luxury items fading to reveal a simple notepad with "RULES" written on it]*

Here's something they don't teach you in school.

There are rules about money. Real rules. Not the ones your parents told you — "save more, spend less, get a good job." Those are fine. But they're not the rules that actually build wealth.

*[Visual: two paths diverging — one leading to a cubicle, one leading to financial freedom]*

The difference between people who retire at 65 with barely enough... and people who build real, generational wealth... isn't intelligence. It isn't luck. It isn't even hard work.

It's the RULES they follow.

Here are 7 money rules that rich people follow — and that no one ever teaches you.

---

### [RULE #1 — PAY YOURSELF FIRST — 0:50 to 2:15]

*[Visual: paycheck with arrows — money going to bills, shopping, and a small amount to savings. Then reverse: savings FIRST]*

**Rule number one: Pay yourself first.**

Most people get their paycheck, pay their bills, buy their stuff, and save whatever is left. Which is usually... nothing.

*[Visual: empty piggy bank]*

Wealthy people flip this completely.

Before they pay rent. Before they buy groceries. Before they do ANYTHING — they take a percentage of their income and put it away. Investments. Savings. Assets.

*[Visual: simple math on screen]*

It doesn't have to be 50%. Start with 10%. If you make $3,000 a month, that's $300. Automatically. Non-negotiable. Like paying a bill to your future self.

Here's the psychology: when the money is GONE before you can spend it, you adjust. You find a way to live on less. But if it's sitting in your checking account, it WILL get spent.

Warren Buffett said it best: "Do not save what is left after spending. Spend what is left after saving."

*[Visual: Buffett quote on screen]*

Set up an automatic transfer the day you get paid. This one rule alone will change your financial life.

---

### [RULE #2 — YOUR INCOME IS NOT YOUR WEALTH — 2:15 to 3:45]

*[Visual: person with luxury car and expensive clothes vs person with modest appearance but growing investment portfolio]*

**Rule number two: Your income is not your wealth.**

This is the trap that catches most people.

You get a raise. What do you do? Upgrade the car. Upgrade the apartment. Upgrade the lifestyle. This is called lifestyle inflation, and it keeps people broke no matter how much they earn.

*[Visual: graph showing income going up, but net worth staying flat]*

There are doctors earning $300,000 a year who are broke. There are teachers earning $50,000 who are millionaires. The difference? The teachers kept their expenses LOW and invested the gap.

*[Visual: simple equation]*

Wealth = What you EARN minus what you SPEND, multiplied by TIME.

A study by Thomas Stanley — the author of "The Millionaire Next Door" — found that the average millionaire in America drives a Toyota, lives in a modest house, and has never bought a suit that costs more than $400.

Your income is a tool. Your wealth is what you DO with that tool.

---

### [RULE #3 — DEBT IS A TOOL, NOT A TRAP — 3:45 to 5:15]

*[Visual: two types of debt shown side by side — credit card (red, bad) vs mortgage/business loan (green, good)]*

**Rule number three: Debt is a tool, not a trap.**

Most people hear "debt" and think: BAD. Dangerous. Avoid at all costs.

And for consumer debt — credit cards, car loans, buy-now-pay-later — they're absolutely right. That kind of debt makes OTHER people rich.

*[Visual: credit card interest compounding]*

But here's what wealthy people understand: there is such a thing as GOOD debt.

Good debt is money you borrow to acquire ASSETS — things that put money BACK in your pocket.

*[Visual: examples]*

A mortgage on a rental property that generates $2,000/month in rent? Good debt.
A business loan that helps you scale from $5,000/month to $50,000/month? Good debt.
A $50,000 car that loses 20% of its value the second you drive it off the lot? Bad debt.

The formula is simple: if the borrowed money makes MORE money than it costs you in interest — it's a tool. If it doesn't — it's a trap.

Rich people use debt strategically. Poor people use debt emotionally.

---

### [RULE #4 — TIME IS WORTH MORE THAN MONEY — 5:15 to 6:45]

*[Visual: hourglass on one side, stack of money on the other — the hourglass tips the scale]*

**Rule number four: Time is your most valuable asset. Not money.**

You can always make more money. You cannot make more time.

*[Visual: compound interest graph — exponential curve]*

This rule works on two levels.

Level one: compound interest. If you invest $500 per month starting at age 25, at a 7% average return, you'll have over $1.1 million by age 65. If you start at 35? You'll have $567,000. Same investment. Same return. TEN YEARS of difference = HALF the result.

*[Visual: two investors starting at different ages]*

Level two: your hourly value. If you earn $30 an hour but spend 3 hours doing something you could pay someone $15 an hour to do — you're LOSING money by doing it yourself.

Wealthy people are obsessed with protecting their time. They delegate. They automate. They say NO to things that waste hours.

Ask yourself: "Is this the highest-value use of my time right now?" If the answer is no, either delegate it or eliminate it.

---

### [RULE #5 — THE 1% RULE — 6:45 to 8:15]

*[Visual: 1% daily improvement graph — starts flat, goes exponential after months]*

**Rule number five: The 1% Rule.**

This is the most underrated rule on this list.

If you improve by just 1% every single day... in one year, you'll be 37 TIMES better. Not 365% better. Thirty-seven TIMES.

*[Visual: 1.01^365 = 37.78 on screen]*

That's the power of compound growth applied to SKILLS, not just money.

*[Visual: small daily actions]*

Read 10 pages a day? That's 3,650 pages a year — about 15 books.
Learn one new skill for 30 minutes a day? In a year, you have 180 hours of expertise.
Save $10 a day? That's $3,650 — and if invested, much more.

The wealthy don't make massive changes. They make CONSISTENT small ones.

James Clear, author of Atomic Habits, calls it "the aggregation of marginal gains." It's not sexy. It's not dramatic. But it's the single most reliable path to extraordinary results.

Don't try to change your life in a week. Change it 1% at a time. Every. Single. Day.

---

### [RULE #6 — AUTOMATE EVERYTHING — 8:15 to 9:45]

*[Visual: automated system — money flowing from paycheck to different accounts automatically]*

**Rule number six: Automate your finances.**

Willpower is a finite resource. If you rely on discipline to save, invest, and manage your money — you will fail. Not because you're weak. Because you're human.

*[Visual: monthly automation flow chart]*

Here's what wealthy people do: they set up systems that run WITHOUT them.

The automation stack:
1. Paycheck lands in checking account
2. Automatic transfer: 20% goes to investments (index funds, ETF)
3. Automatic transfer: 10% goes to emergency fund (until 6 months of expenses saved)
4. Automatic bill pay: rent, utilities, subscriptions — all automated
5. What's left = your spending money. No guilt.

*[Visual: "set it and forget it" concept]*

This is called the "pay yourself first" system on autopilot. You decide ONCE how your money should flow, and then you never think about it again.

The best financial plan isn't the most sophisticated one. It's the one that RUNS WITHOUT YOU.

---

### [RULE #7 — NEVER LOOK RICH — 9:45 to 11:00]

*[Visual: flashy person with expensive watch and car vs modest person checking their investment portfolio — the modest person has 10x the net worth]*

**Rule number seven: Never look rich.**

This is the rule that separates the truly wealthy from the merely high-income.

*[Visual: iceberg — visible lifestyle vs hidden net worth]*

The people who LOOK rich are usually the ones spending everything they earn to maintain an image. The people who ARE rich? You'd never guess it.

Sam Walton — founder of Walmart, at one point the richest man in America — drove a beat-up pickup truck until the day he died.

Warren Buffett still lives in the house he bought in 1958 for $31,500.

Mark Zuckerberg wears the same gray t-shirt every day.

*[Visual: stealth wealth concept]*

This is called "stealth wealth." And it's not about being cheap. It's about understanding that every dollar you spend on LOOKING successful is a dollar that's NOT working for you.

A $50,000 watch doesn't make you rich. It makes the watchmaker rich. A $100,000 car doesn't build your wealth. It builds the dealership's wealth.

Real wealth is QUIET. It's invisible. It's sitting in index funds, real estate, and businesses — not on your wrist or in your driveway.

---

### [CONCLUSION — 11:00 to 11:45]

*[Visual: all 7 rules listed on screen, clean typography]*

Let's recap.

Rule 1: Pay yourself first — before bills, before spending, before everything.
Rule 2: Your income isn't your wealth — what you keep and invest is.
Rule 3: Use debt as a tool, not a crutch.
Rule 4: Protect your time above all else.
Rule 5: Improve 1% every day — compound growth is everything.
Rule 6: Automate your finances — remove willpower from the equation.
Rule 7: Never look rich — build wealth quietly.
